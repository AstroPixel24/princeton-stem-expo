Workshop Sessions

Choose from our exciting selection of hands-on STEM workshops. Each session is limited to 25 participants to ensure personalized attention.

Session A (1:00 - 2:00 PM)

Robotics & AI

Build and program simple robots while learning about artificial intelligence and machine learning concepts.

Led by: Dr. Sarah Chen, Princeton Computer Science

Bioengineering

Explore the intersection of biology and engineering through hands-on DNA extraction and analysis.

Led by: Prof. Michael Rodriguez, Bioengineering Dept.

Chemistry Lab

Conduct exciting chemical experiments and learn about molecular structures and reactions.

Led by: Dr. Lisa Park, Chemistry Department

Space Technology

Design and test model rockets while learning about aerospace engineering principles.

Led by: Dr. James Wilson, Aerospace Engineering

Session B (3:00 - 4:00 PM)

Electronics & Coding

Build circuits and program microcontrollers to create interactive electronic projects.

Led by: Alex Thompson, Google Engineer

Environmental Science

Investigate environmental challenges and design sustainable solutions using scientific methods.

Led by: Dr. Maria Garcia, Environmental Studies

Data Science

Analyze real-world datasets using Python and machine learning to uncover patterns and insights.

Led by: Dr. Kevin Zhang, Data Science Institute

Engineering Design

Work in teams to solve engineering challenges using the design thinking process.

Led by: Prof. Rachel Kim, Mechanical Engineering

Competition Details

Mathematics Competition Structure

  • Sprint Round (9:00 - 9:45 AM): 30 problems, 45 minutes, individual competition
  • Target Round (10:00 - 11:00 AM): 8 problems, 60 minutes, individual competition
  • Team Round (2:00 - 2:45 PM): 15 problems, 45 minutes, teams of 4 students
  • Countdown Round (4:00 - 4:30 PM): Head-to-head elimination for top 16 students

Divisions

  • Middle School: Grades 6-8
  • High School: Grades 9-12

Scoring

  • Individual scores combine Sprint and Target round results
  • Team scores are separate from individual competition
  • Countdown round determines final top placements
  • Awards given for top 10 in each division, plus team awards

Important Information

What to Bring

  • Photo ID or school identification
  • Calculator (scientific calculator recommended, graphing calculators allowed)
  • Pencils and erasers
  • Notebook for workshops
  • Comfortable clothing for full-day event
  • Water bottle (water stations available)

What We Provide

  • All competition materials and answer sheets
  • Workshop supplies and equipment
  • Lunch and refreshments
  • Name badges and lanyards
  • Event t-shirt
  • Certificates for all participants

Transportation & Parking

  • By Car: Visitor parking available in Lot 20 ($5 fee)
  • By Train: Princeton Junction station with shuttle service
  • By Bus: Coordinated transportation for participating schools
  • Campus Map: Available at registration with building locations

Health & Safety

  • First aid station available in Frist Campus Center
  • Emergency contact information required during registration
  • All food allergies and dietary restrictions will be accommodated
  • Adult supervision required for all middle school participants
